2) POISSON BLENDING

- Inside the mask, gradient considers the object only. Outside the mask, gradient considers the background only.

- Here, I defined bad condition as the cropped object acrosses many kinds of objects. In this example, the object meets tree, cloud, and snow (figure5.). So, it is a little awkward to see.

- On the contrary, the good condition was defined as the cropped objects acrosses only one object. In this example, the object meets only snow (figure7.). So, it is very natural to see.

3) MIXED BLENDING

- Inside the mask, the gradient is chosen from the bigger candidates. The candidates are the object and the background.

- At good condition, its result is more natural than poisson blending (i think). The texture of object follows background.

- At bad condition, its result is more awkward than poisson blending (i think). It looks transparent.
